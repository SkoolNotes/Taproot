Taproot linear algebra Norm

norm, \(\lVert x \rVert\) def

For some \(x \in \RR ^n\), \[\begin{aligned} \lVert x \rVert =\sqrt{x_1^2 + \cdots + x_n^2} \end{aligned}\]

Using the definition of an inner product, norms can be defined for complex vectors in inner product spaces

For \(v \in V\), the norm of \(v\), denoted \(\lVert v \rVert\), is defined by \[\begin{aligned} \lVert v \rVert =\sqrt{\langle v, v \rangle} \end{aligned}\]

properties

\(\lVert v \rVert = 0 \iff v=0\)

\(\lVert \lambda v \rVert = |\lambda | \lVert v \rVert\) for all \(\lambda \in \FF\)

aka euclidean distance

not linear, so we use the dot product to ’inject linearity’
