Problem 3

Enclosure problem

Finding the minimum price:

\[\begin{aligned} a\times b &= 120 \\ p &= 10a + 12b \\ \\ b(a) &= \frac{120}{a} \\ p(a) &= 10a + 12b(a) = 10a + \frac{1440}{a} \\ \frac{d}{dx}[p(a)] &= 10 - \frac{1440}{a^2} \\ 10 - \frac{1440}{a^2} &= 0 \\ 10 &= \frac{1440}{a^2} \\ 1 &= \frac{144}{a^2} \\ a^2 &= 144 \\ a &= 12 \\ \\ b(12) &= \frac{120}{12} \\ &= 10 \\ \\ p(12) &= 10(12) + \frac{1440}{12} \\ &= 120 + 120 \\ &= 240 \\ \end{aligned}\] Price: \(\$240\) Dimensions: \(12\) by \(10\) feet

Finding the maximum price:

As there is no limit as to how long any side can be, it is possible to create a rectangle that is \(120\) square feet in area, but has two infinitely long sides, resulting in an infinitely large price. Therefore, there is no possible maximum price.
